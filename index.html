Why these two specialties?

Cryogenics and FPGA sit at opposite ends of the mechanical spectrum — one is deeply physical, the other deeply digital — yet both are where the most exciting, best-paid, and most future-proof engineering happens.

❄️ Cryogenics

Every rocket that reaches orbit, every MRI machine, and every quantum computer relies on cryogenic engineering. It's a domain where thermodynamics, materials science, and mechanical design collide — and where mechanical engineers lead.

🔲 FPGA

FPGAs let you build custom hardware in software. For an ME moving toward robotics, instrumentation, or aerospace controls, understanding FPGAs unlocks real-time systems that microcontrollers simply can't handle.
