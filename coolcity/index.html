The problem

Cities are heat traps. Dark roofs, dense concrete, and missing vegetation push urban temperatures several degrees above surrounding areas — the Urban Heat Island effect. This drives heat illness, energy demand, and mortality, and it hits the most vulnerable neighborhoods hardest. City planners know cooling helps, but they lack a data-driven way to answer: where is it worst, why, and what single action buys the most relief per rupee?

Why we win: physics + AI, not AI alone

A pure black-box model can memorize that "this pixel is hot" but can't be trusted to predict what happens after you plant trees — that's extrapolation. CoolCity AI constrains the model with the surface energy balance (net radiation = sensible + latent + ground heat flux), so its cooling predictions stay physically plausible. That's the difference between a pretty map and a plan a city can act on.
