Deliverables

🗺️ Heat-stress maps

City-wide hotspot maps at 30 m, blending surface temperature with air temp & humidity into human-relevant heat stress.

🔍 Driver assessment

Quantified, per-neighborhood attribution of what causes the heat — vegetation loss, impervious cover, morphology, atmosphere.

🧠 Validated AI/ML model

A physics-informed model of urban heat dynamics, tested on held-out space and time, with reported accuracy and physics-consistency.

🌳 Scenario evaluation

Simulated effectiveness of greening, cool roofs, albedo, and water bodies — each with an estimated °C reduction.

🎯 Optimal strategy

A ranked plan: intervention type + spatial placement + estimated °C drop + population benefited, under a budget.

💻 Interactive dashboard

A map UI to explore hotspots, drivers, and toggle scenarios — the demo judges can click through.

How we prove it's real (validation)

A cooling recommendation is only worth acting on if the model generalizes. Our validation is deliberately adversarial to our own model.

  • Spatial hold-out: train on some districts, predict entirely unseen ones — tests transfer across the city.
  • Temporal hold-out: train on one satellite pass, predict another date/season — tests robustness over time.
  • Physics consistency: report how well predictions satisfy the surface energy balance, not just fit error.
  • Cross-check vs. physical models: compare cooling estimates against SOLWEIG / InVEST independent baselines.
  • Ground truth: calibrate and sanity-check against CPCB station observations where available.
