The intervention catalog

Four evidence-backed strategies, each tied to a physical mechanism the model understands.

🌳 Urban greening

Adds tree canopy / parks. Mechanism: shading + evapotranspiration (raises latent-heat cooling, LE). Strongest daytime effect; also improves air quality.

↑ NDVI↑ LE

🏠 Cool / green roofs

Reflective coatings or vegetated roofs on buildings. Mechanism: higher albedo + insulation reduces absorbed and re-radiated heat. High impact in dense built-up cores.

↑ albedo↓ Rn

☀️ Albedo / pavement change

Lighter road and surface materials. Mechanism: reflects more shortwave radiation instead of absorbing it. Cheap at scale for large paved zones.

↑ albedo

💧 Water bodies

Ponds, fountains, restored wetlands. Mechanism: evaporative cooling + high thermal mass moderates local temperature, especially evenings.

↑ evaporation↑ thermal mass

How optimization works

Define candidate sites

Every hotspot pixel becomes a candidate for one or more feasible interventions (a roof can't become a lake).

Simulate each counterfactual

Edit the driver layers for a candidate, re-run the physics-informed model, and record the predicted °C reduction — locally and in the surrounding area.

Weight by who it helps

Multiply relief by population and vulnerability (from GHSL) so cooling is directed where human impact is greatest, not just where it's technically easiest.

Optimize under budget

A constrained optimizer (greedy / integer program) selects the intervention mix that maximizes total weighted relief within an area or cost budget.

Output the plan

A ranked, mapped list: intervention type → exact location → estimated °C drop → people benefited.
